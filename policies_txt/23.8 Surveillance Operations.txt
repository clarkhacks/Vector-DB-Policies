SURVEILLANCE OPERATIONS
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish procedures to be followed by
Departmental personnel when conducting surveillance, undercover and decoy operations
and raids.
II. AUTHORIZATION
In order to conduct any surveillance, undercover, decoy or raid operations, officers of this
Command must have authorization from the Chief of Police or his/her designee.
III. SURVEILLANCE, UNDERCOVER AND DECOY OPERATIONS:
A. All surveillance operations shall begin with an analysis of:
1. Organized crime or activities involved,
2. The suspect offender,
3. Associates of the Offender,
4. Patrons of the Offender,
5. Vehicles owned and operated by the suspected offender,
6. The method of operation employed to further the activity, and

7. Potential operational problems or dangers, such as weapons possessed by
individuals.
B. All officers assigned to the surveillance operations shall be familiar with the area to be
observed, to include the type of neighborhood, low-income, middle class, or upper-class
housing, business community, industrial park etc., and frequency of complaints or
criminal activity.
C. The supervisor in charge of the operation shall consider the need to employ false
identification in the event identity of the officer could become a problem. At the
conclusion of the operations and when an arrest is anticipated, the undercover officer
would have some article that identifies him as a police officer.
D. An analysis of the suspect(s), activities and neighborhood shall be conducted during
the preliminary investigation to determine operations needs.
E. As part of an undercover operation, it may be necessary for officers to alter their
appearance to fit the area.
F. Officers who are assigned to undercover operations shall be carefully selected based
on the particular operational needs for the assignment. The following criteria shall be
considered:
1. Prior law enforcement experience,
2. Physical build, fitness and agility, and
3. Willingness to participate.
G. The supervisor or officer charged with the investigation is responsible for the official
report.
IV. COMMUNICATIONS:
A. All communications equipment shall be tested prior to use to ensure batteries are
properly charged or in good working order.
B. The officers utilizing portable radios will use the secured Olivette Channel 5 to
eliminate confusion between the operator and other elements of the police department.
C. In the event of an emergency or other unusual circumstance, the primary frequency
Channel 1 shall be used.

V. RAIDS:
A. All raids will be coordinated through the Bureau of Investigation Commander and
approved by the Chief of Police.
B. The St. Louis County Tactical Operations Unit should be used for building entry
involving potentially hazardous suspects or locations.
C. An officer in charge will be designated. The officer in charge is responsible for:
1. Obtaining information on the building to be raided, including the building floor
plan, all doors, and windows.
2. Coordination with the Tactical Operations Unit.
3. Making assignments to each officer involved in the raid.
4. Briefing all personnel on tactics, communications (including radio channels &
call signs), building layout, equipment and weapons, and a Response to
Resistance or Aggression policy review.
D. All personnel involved will wear distinctive markings or clothing.
E. The supervisor or officer charged with the investigation is responsible for the official
report.
VI. COVERT CAMERAS
A. The objective of covert surveillance camera deployment is to develop investigative
leads by capturing images and videos of crimes in progress and documenting vehicles
and subjects in an area during a given time frame.
B. Covert cameras should be used as a surveillance tool. They can be deployed rapidly in
areas that have limited surveillance video coverage, or to supplement existing
surveillance systems. The facilitation of covert camera deployment and subsequent data
analysis will be the responsibility of the Detective Bureau.
C. Deployment:
1. Deployment location should be based on the following:
 History of crimes in the target area;
 Analysis of the times and dates of previous crimes;
 Likelihood a crime may occur in a location (example: unattended
construction site which has numerous tools and building materials
present);

 Cameras may be deployed in other areas at the discretion of the Chief of
Police;
 Covert cameras may be placed on private property with the permission of
the property owner.
2. To prevent theft or damage, cameras should be placed in a location where they
are hidden from view as much as possible. Olivette Public Works may be enlisted
to assist with camera deployments. Cameras should be retrieved after the
designated deployment time (example: overnight or weekend). The deployment
time should not exceed the listed battery life of the camera.
3. All picture and video files will be downloaded from the camera SD card by the
Detective Bureau. The files will be placed in a designated secure folder. The files
will be analyzed by the Detective Bureau and any evidence will be placed in the
appropriate case file.
D. The Detective Bureau is responsible for maintenance of covert cameras to
include changing of batteries when needed, formatting of memory cards, and
proper storage of the cameras when they are not in use.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
