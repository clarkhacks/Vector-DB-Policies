PATROL RIFLE PROGRAM
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish guidelines for the use of the patrol rifle.
For the purpose of this Order, the term patrol rifle means a Department owned semi-
automatic, center fire, 5.56 caliber Police Carbine model #M4LE or M4 Commando,
manufactured by Colt that has been inspected and approved by an armorer of the Olivette
Police Department to be carried in Olivette police vehicles by sworn, full-time officers.
II. POLICY:
It is the policy of the Olivette Police Department to ensure that sworn officers who
employ patrol rifles are properly trained in its use, have passed a Department approved
qualification course and maintain an acceptable qualification status.
1. Each patrol rifle to be carried on-duty shall have its serial number, make and
model recorded with the Department.
2. Officers will meet the requirements set forth in this policy prior to deploying
the patrol rifle on-duty.

III. ACCESSORIES:
A. Each patrol rifle shall be equipped with a sling.
B. Each patrol rifle shall be equipped with a Department issued Red Dot sight.
C. Each patrol rifle shall be equipped with a Department issued flashlight.
D. Any other accessories must be approved on a case-by-case basis by the Chief of
Police.
IV. PERTINENCE OF OTHER ORDERS:
Officers using the patrol rifles shall adhere to the applicable policies set forth in various
areas of this manual pertaining to the Response to Resistance or Aggression and
qualifications, including but not limited to:
1. Deadly and Less than Lethal Response to Resistance or Aggression,
2. Weapons, and
3. Firearms.
V. TRAINING AND QUALIFICATION COURSE:
The qualification course shall be approved by a Department certified Firearms Instructor.
A. Prior to use of the patrol rifle on-duty, Patrol Officers shall attend a Department
approved patrol rifle training class to include lessons in weapons safety, patrol rifle
familiarization, a combat-type course of fire, and a Department approved patrol rifle
standard marksmanship qualification course.
B. The Patrol Officer’s attendance at an initial patrol rifle training class may be waived
upon recommendation by a Department certified Firearm’s Instructor and approval made
by the Chief of Police. This recommendation shall be based upon consideration of the
Patrol Officer’s prior knowledge, experience, and training with a rifle equivalent to the
Department issued patrol rifle. If the training class is waived, the Patrol Officer must
successfully demonstrate proficiency at an initial Department approved patrol rifle
standard marksmanship qualification course.
C. Subsequent qualifications will be completed in conjunction with the normal firearm’s
qualifications and shall be a Department Approved standard marksmanship course.
D. If an officer fails to qualify, he/she is responsible for notifying their immediate
supervisor and through the chain of command the Deputy Chief shall:

1. Arrange for the officer to receive the appropriate training to correct the problem
causing the deficiency.
2. Coordinate the re-scheduling of the officer for firearms qualification within
thirty (30) days from the first scheduled date.
3. If an officer does not receive a passing score within thirty (30) days, the officer
may not be authorized to carry the patrol rifle or serve as a commissioned officer
of this Department as determined by the Chief of Police.
VI. CARRYING OF PATROL RIFLES:
A. Patrol Rifles shall be carried in the lockable vehicle rack of the police vehicle in the
car-ready position and shall only be deployed when deemed necessary in situations
stipulated further in this Order.
B. The car-ready position will be a thirty (30) round PMAG loaded to capacity with
Department-issued ammunition in the magazine well, chamber empty, and the rifle
selector switch in the "SAFE" position.
VII. DEPARTMENTAL RESPONSIBILITIES:
To ensure efficient and reliable operation when needed, an Olivette Police Department
Armorer will always maintain the weapon in good working order.
1. While being carried on-duty, each weapon is subject to inspection by the on-
duty supervisor, Department Rifle Instructors, or Department Armorers.
2. Any weapon found to be dirty and/or not properly maintained will be
immediately removed from service until such time as the weapon is brought into
serviceable order and re-inspected by a Department Armorer.
VIII. DEPLOYMENT:
A. As it is impossible to set down in writing every possible scenario, circumstance or set
of facts, in which a patrol rifle should or could be deployed, this section is to serve as a
guideline regarding the deployment of patrol rifles.
B. Examples of situations in which a patrol rifle could be deployed include but are not
limited to situations where the suspect(s) may be:
1. Wearing/utilizing body armor or any other device that would defeat, impede or
otherwise inhibit the use of a handgun.
2. Armed with or has immediate access to a high-powered weapon that surpasses
the weapons normally carried by field personnel.

3. Armed and situated in a distant or fortified location which affords the
suspects(s) a tactically superior position, in which the deployment of a patrol rifle
may be deemed necessary to neutralize any threat posed by the suspect(s).
4. Armed and/or barricaded where the officer is manning a perimeter position.
5. Exigent circumstances where the on-duty supervisor deems appropriate.
C. As a rule, when articulable circumstances exist that indicate the deployment of the
patrol rifle would greatly enhance the officer’s ability to prevent death or serious harm to
themselves or others, the rifle may be deployed.
IX. DEADLY RESPONSE TO RESISTANCE OR AGGRESSION:
A. Officers should be aware that even though the specialty ammunition authorized by the
Olivette Police Department decreases the risk of the .223 caliber round over-penetrating,
it does not decrease the over-all range of the round in the event of a missed shot. Officers
should evaluate the surrounding area and the risks a missed shot might pose to persons in
the area.
B. Nothing in this Order shall supersede the established Response to Resistance or
Aggression policy outlined in General Order 9.1.
X. ANNUAL TRAINING
The Department will provide in-service patrol rifle training to officers annually. The in-
service training will be conducted by certified instructors. The firearms instructor will
forward a copy of training attendance to the Deputy Chief for recording in the officer’s
training records.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
