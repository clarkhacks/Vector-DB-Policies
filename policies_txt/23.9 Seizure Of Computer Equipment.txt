SEIZURE OF COMPUTER EQUIPMENT
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish procedures to be followed by
Department personnel when seizing computer equipment as evidence.
II. SEIZURE OF COMPUTER EQUIPMENT:
A. A computer is defined as an electronic device that stores numerical data and written
information, and which provides logical high-speed answers to questions in regard to the
data contained therein. Such devices may also serve as a link to other computer sites and
provide a means of electronic communication via e-mail.
B. Computer equipment is defined as the hardware (mainframe, keyboard, monitor,
modem, and printer) and the software (programs stored on internal or external media) that
comprise modern computer operations.
C. When a seizure of computer equipment is deemed necessary, field investigators,
detectives, or other uniformed officers at the scene shall note the operating status of the
equipment, which shall influence seizure proceedings.
1. Should the computer system be off and its parts disconnected from one another,
and a power source, the area shall be photographed, and the equipment may be
removed and packaged.
2. Should a computer system be connected to other unit components and a power
source, the scene shall be secured from unauthorized access, and the system and
surrounding area shall be photographed. No attempt shall be made by untrained
personnel to access the system or otherwise power same down. Trained personnel

shall then be requested to respond to the scene to manage the seizure proceedings.
The computer equipment will be powered down, disassembled, and seized.
III. PROCEDURAL STEPS FOR SEIZURE:
In an effort to maximize the potential for meaningful data retrieval, the following
procedural steps have been established regarding the physical seizure of computer
equipment:
1. Photograph the general scene, computer monitor (including any on-screen
display), and electrical connections.
2. Power system down and disconnect from electrical outlets.
3. Label all connectors prior to disassembly.
4. Seize all available software and computer disks.
5. Keep hardware units and connectors together.
6. Record equipment identification numbers and descriptions.
7. Take hardware/software to the Police Department.
8. Package software and tag hardware.
9. Complete evidence sheet(s) and store items in a secure and cool environment.
IV. PROCEDURAL STEPS FOR SEIZURE OF MOBILE DEVICES:
In an effort to maximize the potential for meaningful data retrieval, the following
procedural steps have been established regarding the physical seizure of mobile devices:
1. If the device is “off”, do not turn “on”.
2. If the device is “on”, do not turn “off”.
3. Photograph device and screen display, if available.
4. Label and collect all cables, including power supply.
5. Take device and cables to Police Department.
6. Keep device charged.
7. Complete evidence documentation.

8. If device cannot be kept charged, analysis by a specialist must be completed prior
to battery discharge.
V. SEARCH/START-UP PROCESS:
The Bureau of Investigations personnel will contact the United States Secret Service – St.
Louis Office – for forensic computer/mobile device services.
VI. LEGAL CONSULTATION:
In those instances where a question exists as to the legal justification regarding the
seizure of computer components, mobile devices, or documentation derived there from,
consultation should be made with the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office
prior to any seizure being initiated.
Information that may be obtained by a search of the computer equipment or mobile
devices will be secured and documented as evidence according to the situation. Other
data will be held with the computer until released by proper authorization.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
