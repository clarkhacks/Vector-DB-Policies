RECRUITMENT PLAN
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of the recruitment plan is to create more diversity in ethnic and gender
staffing. The Plan will establish guidelines that will assist the Department in their efforts
to diversify.
II. RESPONSIBILITY
The responsibility of the recruitment program will belong to the Deputy Chief. As
coordinator, the Deputy Chief will work in concert with the Recruitment Officer(s) and
the City’s Human Resources Administrator to maintain, revise and implement the
Department’s recruitment plan. The team will make their recommendations to the Chief
of Police and the City Manager.
III. GOALS/OBJECTIVES
1. To obtain the caliber of qualified personnel necessary to provide the public
with professional law enforcement services;
2. To increase the recruitment of minority and female applicants to better reflect
the demographics of the City of Olivette;
3. To better acquaint members of the Department with its recruitment objectives
and to motivate all members to become actively involved in the Department’s
recruitment efforts.
IV. RECRUITMENT
The Department will maintain a continuous recruitment effort throughout the year
through the following steps:
 Maintain a 12-month eligibility list to fill vacancies as they become
available.

 Develop relationships with civic organizations, educational
institutions, including local P.O.S.T certified law enforcement training
academies, and key community leaders to provide recruitment
assistance, referrals and advice about the Department.
 Create new ways to disseminate job announcements in addition to the
City website, print media and local educational institutions.
 Participate in local diversity job fairs for police recruitment.
 Volunteer to host one of the job fairs annually.
This list is not meant to be all inclusive and will continue to develop each year based on
the annual analysis.
V. ANNUAL ANALYSIS
Recruitment goals and objectives will be evaluated annually to determine the success of
the Department’s recruitment efforts. An “Annual Analysis” of the Department’s
recruitment plan will be completed at the end of each calendar year and submitted to the
Chief of Police. The annual analysis will detail the Department’s progress toward stated
goals and provide demographic data of both sworn personnel and the community. The
analysis will also make recommendations for revisions to the current recruitment plan, if
necessary.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
