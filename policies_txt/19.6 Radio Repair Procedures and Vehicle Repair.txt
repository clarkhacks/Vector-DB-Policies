RADIO REPAIR PROCEDURES AND VEHICLE REPAIR
I. PURPOSE
It is the purpose of this General Order to establish procedures for the repair of police
vehicles and radios. The Day Watch supervisor is responsible for ensuring that all
vehicle repairs are accomplished.
II. VEHICLE STATUS
Any police vehicles needing repair will have a repair work order completed. The vehicle
repair work order will be placed on the designated bulletin board in the Squad Room.
III. VEHICLE BREAK DOWN – MECHANICAL FAILURE
When a vehicle breaks down as a result of mechanical failure (i.e. transmission, won't
start, etc.) a vehicle repair order will be prepared listing the problem. If it is during the
hours that Public Works is open, the vehicle, along with the vehicle repair order, will be
taken to the garage and garage personnel will perform the repair work if available. If
garage personnel are unable to repair the vehicle, it will be taken to a designated repair
facility per the Deputy Chief.
IV. VEHICLE BREAK DOWN – AFTER NORMAL WORKING HOURS
A. If the vehicle should break down after normal working hours, it will be “dead-lined”
and noted on the designated bulletin board. The Day Watch Supervisor will then assign
an officer to convey the vehicle and repair order to the garage for repair.
V. FLAT TIRES

A. Flat tires (during normal business hours and at the Police Department) will be
changed by Public Works. Flat tires at other times and locations may be changed by an
appropriate tow company.
B. Vehicles needing to be towed may be towed by an appropriate tow company.
VI. VEHICLE RADIOS, EMERGENCY LIGHT SYSTEMS, SIRENS, OR FLASHING
HEADLIGHTS MALFUNCTIONS OR BREAK DOWNS
When a vehicle's radio, emergency light system, siren, or flashing headlights
malfunction, a repair order shall be prepared. If the break down occurs during normal
business hours (7:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.) the repair order will be forwarded through the
proper chain of command to the Deputy Chief. The appropriate private contractor will
then be contacted for service. Should the break down occur after normal working hours,
the vehicle will be “dead-lined” and noted on the ECDC Deskbook. The Day Watch
Supervisor will prepare a vehicle repair order and it will be forwarded through the proper
chain of command, so that contact can be made with the appropriate vendor.
VII. PORTABLE RADIO REPAIR:
If a portable radio malfunctions, an Olivette memorandum shall be used. On the
memorandum, list the serial number of the radio, the serial number of the attached
battery, and the problem. The memorandum, along with the portable radio and battery,
will be placed in the Deputy Chief’s mailbox so that contact can be made with the
appropriate vendor.
VIII. PARKING
At the Police Department, marked units will park on the East side of the gated parking
lot, with the front of the vehicle facing West.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
