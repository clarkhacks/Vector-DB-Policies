COURT BONDS
I. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this General Order is to establish the types of bonds accepted by the
Department and procedures to be followed when accepting.
II. PRISONER RELEASE:
All prisoners incarcerated are the responsibility of the Department until such time as they
are released.
III. MUNICIPAL COURT BONDS:
A. If the arrest is based on a warrant, the bond amount is listed on the warrant. If the bond
amount is not listed on the warrant, the arresting officer will contact the Municipal Court
for clarification. If the arrest is not based on a warrant, and the arresting officer believes
there is cause to hold the prisoner for a bond, a Municipal Court Warrant Application
form and a Municipal Court Probable Cause Statement form will be completed and
submitted to the Municipal Court and Municipal Prosecutor for consideration.
B. Only cash and credit cards will be accepted.
C. Persons posting bonds during court business hours will be directed to the court
window.
D. Monies collected from cash bonds after business hours will be forwarded to the
Municipal Court. The officer that receives the bond money will place the money and
associated paperwork in an envelope and secure it inside the bond lockbox. After
depositing the money, the officer will notify the Court Administrator of its receipt via
voicemail.

E. Officers accepting a municipal court bond will issue a receipt to the person posting the
bond. The receipt will indicate if the bond was paid by cash or credit card.
F. After accepting a bond, the officer will log the transaction(s) in the bond accounting
spreadsheet.
G. If a bond is posted during normal Court business hours, the Court will provide the on-
duty Watch Commander a copy of the receipt/bond paperwork. The Watch Commander,
or designee, will access the spreadsheet and complete it accordingly.
H. The Bureau of Support Services Commander will reconcile the total amount of bonds
accepted by the Police Department with the Court Administrator quarterly.
IV. BONDS FOR OTHER JURISDICTIONS:
A. Officers can accept bond for other jurisdictions as directed by the shift commander.
B. Only money orders or cashier’s check for the exact amount of the bond made payable
to the appropriate court or agency (not the City of Olivette) can be accepted. Do not
accept cash for other jurisdictions.
C. Obtain the correct court date, court name and address and complete Form #OPD –
F1008. (Appearance Bond for Other Agency).
D. The cashier’s check or money order and the original bond form will be placed in
Evidence Locker 3. A voice message and/or an email will be sent to the Evidence
Custodian advising to retrieve the bond and deliver it to the Records Clerk for mailing to
the designated agency.
E. The officer accepting the bond will notify ECDC to send a REJIS message to the other
jurisdiction when the person is released. Attach a copy of the REJIS message to the
booking sheet.
V. RELEASE OF PROPERTY:
A. Prisoners shall be requested to sign the receipt for their personal property after items
have been compared against the original inventory log and found complete.
B. Any discrepancies shall be reported immediately to the shift commander or supervisor.
C. Refusal to sign for property shall be noted by the releasing officer.
D. If the prisoner is transferred to another agency, the property will be signed for by the
accepting officer.
VI. RELEASE OF INFORMATION:

Authorities taking custody of released prisoners shall be provided with all relevant
information on the prisoner, to include information on pending charges, illnesses, injury,
suicide attempts or potentials, drug use, use of prescription drugs, medical treatment
records or diagnosis and potential for violence or escape.
VII. RELEASE PENDING APPLICATION OF WARRANT:
Suspects booked on State charges may be released Pending Application of Warrant
(PAW) through the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
