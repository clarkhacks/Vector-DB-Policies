COMPUTERIZED VOICE STRESS ANALYZER
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish procedures for the use of the
Department’s Computerized Voice Stress Analyzer.
II. POLICY
A. The Olivette Police Department has established guidelines for administering
Computer Voice Stress Analysis examinations in conjunction with pre-employment
screening, internal affairs investigations and criminal investigations conducted by the
police department.
B. Computer Voice Stress Analysis examinations by qualified examiners are an
information aid and tool to be used in conjunction with an investigation or the pre-
employment process. However, they should not be used as a single determinant for
offering employment or a final determinant factor in investigations. They should not
circumvent good investigative procedures.
C. The Computer Voice Stress Analysis is used for truth verification. Therefore, the
Computer Voice Stress Analysis should be viewed as a means to protect the integrity of
an investigation and/or the interviewee.
III. DEFINITIONS
A. COMPUTERIZED VOICE STRESS ANALYZER (CVSA) – detects, measures and
charts the stress in a person’s voice following a pre-formatted questionnaire.

B. QUALIFIED EXAMINER – A person who has satisfactorily completed training by a
recognized instructor in truth verification and the use of the CVSA. In addition,
recertification must be successfully completed every two (2) years.
C. OVERT INTERVIEW – A live interview by a CVSA examiner with a suspect, victim
witness, complainant, employee, or perspective employee. These interviews are
conducted with prior knowledge and permission that certain questions will be recorded
live and captured by the CVSA for analysis.
D. STRUCTURED INTERVIEW – A legally obtained audio tape interview of a suspect,
victim, witness, or complainant. The interviews are designed to capture a response to
preformatted questions. This taped interview is then analyzed by the CVSA examiner.
IV. PROCEDURE
A. Use of CVSA examination for pre-employment screening:
CVSA examination will be used in the selection process for employment with the police
department. Prior to the CVSA exam with police department applicants, a CVSA
questionnaire packet will be completed by the applicant. The CVSA examiner will
review the questions with the applicant, prior to the examination.
B. Use of CVSA examination for investigative screening:
CVSA examination should be utilized in conjunction with investigative leads and
interviews of available suspect/s, victim/s, and/or witnesses. These results are not to be
used for arrest or legal action, but are designed for developing leads and/or obtaining case
direction.
C. Persons who may be tested:
1. Any individual who knows right from wrong. Generally juveniles who
recognize right from wrong may be tested when prior approval has been received
from the St. Louis County Family Court.
2. Tests on juveniles will be conducted at the discretion of the examiner, subject
to prior approval from the St. Louis County Family Court.
D. Persons who may not be tested:
1. Any person who has been forced or coerced into taking the examination.
2. Any person who has been indicted by a Grand Jury or formally charged for the
crime about which the CVSA is being requested, unless there is an agreement and
stipulation signed by the person to be examined, his or her attorney, and the St.
Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office.

E. Responsibilities of CVSA Examiner:
1. The CVSA Examiner will review all the available information pertaining to the
case in question prior to administering an examination.
2. The Chief of Police, Deputy Chief and Commander of the Bureau of
Investigations must be advised of any structured interview using the CVSA.
3. The Chief of Police, Deputy Chief and Commander of the Bureau of
Investigations or designee must approve an examination request of another
organization prior to conducting the CVSA examination.
4. All CVSA examiners will maintain a record of all examinations they have
conducted.
5. The CVSA examiner can receive a second opinion on examinations from
another certified examiner if necessary.
6. The CVSA examiner will refrain from examinations that may compromise his
integrity. Any tests of friends, relatives or persons the examiner has a relationship
with which represents a conflict of interest must be conducted by a neutral
examiner.
F. Responsibilities of Detective requesting a CVSA examination:
1. The detective will initiate an investigation and consult with the examiner prior
to a CVSA examination being scheduled. The CVSA is a supplement to, not a
substitute for, a thorough investigation.
2. The detective is responsible for notifying the subject of the date and time for
the scheduled appointment.
3. The detective will notify the CVSA examiner immediately if the subject
cancels an examination appointment.
G. CVSA Records
1. CVSA records include a waiver of rights(if necessary), truth verification
release, CVSA graphs, and a statement of results.
2. CVSA records will be maintained in accordance with Missouri laws and
statutes.

3. The Commander of the Bureau of Investigations or designee is responsible for
the storage of the CVSA records.
4. The CVSA examiner will write a supplemental report or memorandum
concerning the results of the test and interview.
H. Internal Affairs
1. CVSA examinations may be administered during criminal investigations
focusing on a sworn officer or civilian member only if the member freely
volunteers to participate in the examination. This ensures the member’s
constitutional rights and permits any statements or admissions made during the
examination to be admitted as evidence.
2. CVSA examinations may be administered during administrative investigations.
3. Civilian complaints or witnesses may be tested with the CVSA in order to
determine if their complaint, allegation or knowledge of a case is legitimate.
These interviews must only be conducted after a voluntary test waiver has been
signed.
In case of a structured interview (audio tape analysis), the examiner will receive
authority from the Deputy Chief.
4. A CVSA examination will not be the sole determinant of an internal affairs
investigation conclusion.
I. CVSA examinations may be administered to fire department, civilian and volunteer
applicants as well as persons having access to restricted areas of the Police Department.
This is to ensure the following:
1. Suitability for employment;
2. Verify accuracy and completeness of information on the application;
3. Resolve questions or conflicts arising during background investigations;
4. Discover previous criminal or other disqualifying behavior; and
5. Deter those seeking to penetrate law enforcement departments for improper
purposes.
J. The CVSA will not be the single determinant of employment status. However,
admissions made before, during or after the examination may be used to show cause.
