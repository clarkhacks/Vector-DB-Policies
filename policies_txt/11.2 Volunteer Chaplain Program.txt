VOLUNTEER CHAPLAIN PROGRAM
I. POLICY
The Volunteer Chaplain Program is intended to support the mission of the Police
Department in providing a source of strength to the law enforcement officers and their
families, other department members and the community. The program will support
officers in the handling of crisis situations, comforts victims and their families when
accidents occur and criminal incidents are committed and provides counseling and other
ministerial functions that may be needed.
II. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish operating procedures, guidelines, duties
and responsibilities of the Volunteer Chaplain Program for the Olivette Police
Department and the City of Olivette.
III. PROCEDURES
A. Definitions
CHAPLAINCY - A volunteer ecumenical ministry to the citizens of Olivette and
personnel of the Olivette Police Department.
CHAPLAIN COORDINATOR - A member of the Olivette Police Department designated
by the Chief of Police, who will be responsible for the Volunteer Chaplain Program.
B. Qualifications/ Characteristics
1. Experience

Must manifest a broad base of experience and professional ministry, emotional
stability and personal flexibility. S/he should have a minimum of five (5) years
experience in ministry or professional care giving.
2. Certified
Be an ecclesiastically certified person in good standing and endorsed for the law
enforcement chaplaincy by a recognized religious body.
3. Committed to Duties
Be willing and available to respond to any and all situations where a chaplain's
presence is indicated.
4. High Moral and Spiritual Standards
Must show a God-like compassion, understanding and love for others and relates
easily to people. Maintain high spiritual moral standards and be tactful and
considerate in approaching all people, regardless of race, sex, creed or religion.
C. Chain of Command
1. Chief of Police
2. Deputy Chief
3. Chaplain Coordinator
D. General Duties
1. Respond to assignments made by the Police Department supervisor and/or
when contacted by the dispatcher.
2. Assist officers in the making of death notifications or such other notifications
as requested.
3. Assist at scene of trauma in counseling and assisting victims, friends and/or
family members as requested by officers.
4. Assist in such ways as needed and requested by law enforcement personnel.
5. Perform and assist in memorial services.
6. Visit Olivette Police Department personnel and their families while in the
hospital.

7. Assist in other training as requested, including basic training and in-service for
both sworn and civilian members.
E. Complaints
Complaints against the chaplain shall follow the same procedures as set forth for the
employees of the Olivette Police Department. All reports shall be forwarded through the
chain of command to the Chief of Police.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
