PROMOTION PROCEDURES
I. POLICY STATEMENT
A. The promotion process implemented in this General Order is designed to:
1. Establish criteria for selecting commissioned officers qualified to fill vacancies
for promotion to the rank of Major, Lieutenant, Sergeant, and Corporal.
2. Create standards and measurements that will assist in promoting candidates that
meet the criteria for advancement for each of these positions.
3. Encourage employees to pursue higher education.
B. The City of Olivette Human Resources Administrator will administer promotional
processes.
II. MAJOR
A. APPLICATION AND QUALIFICATIONS
1. A written job announcement for the promotional opening(s) will be posted by
the Human Resources Administrator. All candidates must submit the required
documents to the Human Resources Administrator by the date listed in the job
announcement.
2. All candidates for promotion must meet the following qualifications at the time
of application to be considered for the position.
• Minimum of ten (10) years of law enforcement experience, preferred.
• Minimum of three (3) years of supervisory experience with the Olivette
Police Department.
• Bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university; Master’s
Degree from an accredited college or university preferred.

B. PROMOTION PROCESS
1. Chief’s Interview
The Chief of Police will review all documents submitted by the candidates. Each
candidate will be interviewed by the Chief of Police.
C. SELECTION
When the promotional process has been completed, the Chief of Police will
choose a candidate to be promoted to the rank of Major and submit his/her
selection to the City Manager for approval. Upon receiving approval, the Chief of
Police will make an announcement naming the candidate to be promoted.
III. LIEUTENANT
A. APPLICATION AND QUALIFICATIONS
1. A written job announcement for the promotional opening(s) will be posted by
the Human Resources Administrator. All candidates must submit the required
documents to the Human Resources Administrator by the date listed in the job
announcement.
2. All candidates for promotion must meet the following qualifications at the time
of application to be considered for the position.
• Minimum of eight (8) years of law enforcement experience, preferred.
• Minimum of two (2) years of supervisory experience with the Olivette
Police Department.
• Bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university.
• Must have an overall average score equivalent to “Meets Expectations” on
the most recently completed annual performance evaluation.

2. Candidates must achieve the passing percentage on each component to advance
in the process.
3. The Human Resources Administrator will be responsible for notifying each
candidate, in writing, of their score after completion of each section.
C. PROMOTION PROCESS
1. Panel Interview
The panel will be selected by the Chief of Police and shall consist of a minimum
of three (3) members outside of the Department. The panel will be provided with
a minimum of five (5) questions by the Chief of Police. The point total scored by
each member of the panel will be averaged to determine the panel's final point
total for the candidate.
2. Chief’s Interview
The Chief of Police will review all documents submitted by the candidates. Each
candidate will be interviewed by the Chief of Police and Deputy Chief of Police.
D. SELECTION
When the promotional process has been completed, scores will be totaled. The
Chief of Police will choose from the top three (3) candidates and submit his/her
selection(s) to the City Manager for approval. Upon receiving approval, the Chief
of Police will make an announcement naming the candidate to be promoted.

• Minimum of five (5) years of law enforcement experience, preferred.
• Minimum of one (1) year of supervisory experience with the Olivette
Police Department, preferred.
• Associate’s degree from an accredited college or university; Bachelor’s
degree from an accredited college or university preferred.
• Must have an overall average score equivalent to “Meets Expectations” on
the most recently completed annual performance evaluation.

VI. PROMOTIONAL PROBATION
The probationary period for all promotional employees shall commence immediately
upon promotion and shall continue for a period of one (1) year.
VII. PROMOTIONAL PROCESS VARIANCE
If the number of qualified promotional candidates is equal to the number of promotional
openings, step(s) of a promotional process may be waived at the discretion of the Chief of
Police.
