CHILDREN'S ADVOCACY
I. PURPOSE:
A. The Children’s Advocacy Centers of St. Louis County represent a community-wide
effort to address the immediate needs of children identified as possible victims of sexual
abuse and severe physical abuse by sensitively providing objective interviews and
medical evaluations necessary for determining appropriate interventions.
B. The purpose of this Directive is to establish Department policy regarding the
utilization of the Child Advocacy Center during the investigation of child sexual abuse
and/or severe physical abuse cases.
II. REFERRAL CRITERIA:
A. A referral for evaluation for the Children’s Advocacy Center must be received from
the Missouri Children’s Division or Law Enforcement Officials and meet the following
requirements:
1. Be a child who is between the ages of 3 and 18;
2. There are allegations that the child was a victim of or witness to: sexual abuse,
physical abuse, neglect, witness of abuse/violent crime, and the crime was
allegedly perpetrated within the city of Olivette.
B. In suspected multi-victim cases, the number of children evaluated may be limited and
such limitations shall be established in consultation with Missouri Children’s Division
and Law Enforcement Officials.
C. Cases that do not fit the above criteria should be handled through Department-
approved investigative techniques.
D. If deemed necessary by investigative process, the Children’s Advocacy Center may
also accept requests for interviews of other individuals on a case-by-case basis, including
but not limited to:

1. A child under the age of three (3) if deemed appropriate;
2. An adult with a developmental disability who would best be served through the
use of interview techniques utilized by the CAC;
3. Situations in which a child has recanted a previous disclosure;
4. Concerns of a child being a victim of human trafficking;
5. A child who has witnessed a non-violent crime;
6. A child who is displaying developmentally inappropriate sexualized behavior for
the purpose of determining whether they have been a victim of sexual abuse;
7. Concerns of child chronic neglect.
8. Concerns of a child being a victim of commercial sexual exploitation;
9. Concerns of a child being a victim of or exposed to child sexual abuse material.
I. PROCEDURE
Upon completion of the initial investigation and based upon the officers' reasonable cause
to believe that a child sexual abuse may have taken place, the investigating officer will:
1. Immediately notify the Children’s Division, via the Child Abuse/Neglect Hotline,
number 1-800-392-3738.
2. Prepare a CARE Report containing all necessary information.
3. If necessary, take the child into protective custody and remove the child from the
setting to prevent imminent danger.
4. Contact the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office and advise the
Prosecutor in charge of the Sexual Assault Team of the circumstances.
5. Contact the Children’s Advocacy Center within twenty-four hours to arrange an
interview. Interviews should be scheduled as soon as possible of the initial report
or in accordance with the CAC schedule.
6. Contact the Prosecuting Attorney assigned to the case to advise the date and time
of the interview.
7. Continue investigation prior to a CAC appointment.
a) Interview of witnesses other than the child victim including the non-
offending parent when applicable.

b) Interview perpetrator when deemed necessary.
c) Collect evidence, take photos, and make investigative judgments and
action as appropriate.
8. Provide transportation of the child and non-offending parent, if applicable, to the
Children’s Advocacy Center if needed. Remain for the entire assessment
including observation of the interview. The Police Officer, Children’s Division
caseworker and Prosecuting Attorney all review the in-depth interview conducted
by CAC’s Interview Specialist. Facilitate returning the child and parent home if
needed.
9. Complete all further investigations, suspect interviews and arrest when deemed
necessary. Refer original and supplementary reports and warrant application to
the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office as soon as possible.
