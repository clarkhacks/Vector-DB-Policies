NEWS RELEASES
I. PURPOSE:
Information regarding the activities and performance of the Department to the public will
be provided through the facilities of print, broadcast and electronic media inquiries. It
recognizes that the responsibility for protecting the constitutional guarantees of both fair
trial and free press is shared equally by police and media.
II. NEWS RELEASES:
The Olivette Police Department shall issue a written news release concerning Department
activity when deemed appropriate by the Chief of Police.
1. When a news release is issued, the employee issuing the release will ensure that
the release is made equally available to all legitimate news media representatives.
2. The format of news releases will be in written form and will be approved by the
Chief of Police except in exigent circumstances.
3. When available, the Chief of Police or his/her designee should be utilized to
compose and disseminate news releases and to set up news conferences.
4. After a written release has been made, the person making the release will, as
soon as possible, forward or deliver a copy of the release to the Chief of Police,
and any supervisors who are affected by the content of the news release.
5. All news releases shall be printed on official Olivette Police Department
stationary. In most circumstances, release should contain the "who, what, when
where and why" of the event being reported, in accordance with all Operational
Guidelines. In addition, the release should also contain the date the release was
issued, a name and phone number for media representatives to contact for further
information, and the name of the person who authorized the release.

6. Supervisors other than the Chief of Police may make or authorize a written
news release in the following circumstances:
a) Road conditions made hazardous by snow, ice or flooding
b) To announce lengthy, but temporary traffic detours due to traffic
accidents or other traffic hazards.
c) Routine information regarding fatality traffic accidents.
d) To advise of malfunctioning traffic signals.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
