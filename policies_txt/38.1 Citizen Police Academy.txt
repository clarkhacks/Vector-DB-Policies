CITIZEN POLICE ACADEMY
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to provide an understanding to Department
employees of this Agency’s participation in the multi-jurisdictional West County Citizen
Police Academy (CPA). This General Order only applies to personnel and procedures of
the Olivette Police Department.
II. ACADEMY STAFF
A. Only commissioned officers will be assigned as dedicated liaisons to the CPA.
B. Those officers assigned to the CPA will be chosen from the Bureau of Field
Operations.
C. The Chief of Police will select the CPA liaisons.
D. Officers assigned to the CPA will be responsible for participating in course
development and applicant screening.
E. While instructing at the CPA, officers will wear the uniform of the day or other
approved clothing which identifies officers as members of the Olivette Police
Department.
III. SELECTION PROCESS FOR PARTICIPANTS
A. Citizens who want to attend the CPA must submit a completed application form.
B. In order to be accepted for a class, applicants must pass a background investigation.
C. Applicants are not required to be citizens of the City of Olivette, MO, however,
applicants who reside within the municipalities who participate in the CPA will be given
seat priority over non-residents.

D. A wait list will be made if seats for the CPA class have been filled. Priority for filling
seats from the wait list will be for residents first.
E. An Advanced Citizens Police Academy is available only for graduates of the basic
CPA of any previous year. The selection process is the same for the Advanced CPA as it
is for the basic CPA.
IV. CURRICULUM
A. Topics to be covered in the CPA include, but are not limited to:
1. Constitutional Law,
2. Statutory Law,
3. Response to Resistance or Aggression/Defensive Tactics,
4. Traffic Enforcement/Accident Investigation,
5. Pursuits/DWI Enforcement,
6. Investigations,
7. Police Weaponry & Firearms Safety, and
8. Firearms Range & Role Play.
B. Topics to be covered in the Advanced CPA include, but are not limited to:
1. K-9 Unit Practices & Procedures,
2. Computer Crimes,
3. Major Case Squad,
4. Metro Air Support & St. Louis County Tactical Operations Unit.
B. In order that the most qualified officers are teaching the curriculum, officers other than
the designated liaisons may be assigned to instruct individual topics.
V. EQUIPMENT
A. Certain pieces of Departmental equipment may be utilized for instructional purposes
at the discretion of the Chief of Police.
