SUMMONS PREPARATION
I. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this General Order is to establish procedures for filling out a Missouri Uniform
Complaint and Summons.
II. PREPARATION:
The Mobile Ticketing program will be the primary system for issuing summonses, citations and
written warnings. In the event the Mobile Ticketing system is not functioning, officers will
manually complete a Missouri Uniform Complaint and Summons from the department-issued
triplicate ticket book. The procedures will be as follows:
III. MOBILE TICKETING - CITATIONS:
A. Log-in to the system utilizing the officer’s REJIS identification number and password.
B. Verify that the shift settings are current.
C. When entering a new ticket, choose the violation type (Local Ordinance) and then
appropriate Ticket Type from the drop-down menu (Citation OR Parking)
D. Start on the “Basics” tab. Required fields are labeled according to REJIS standards but
available information should be entered.
E. Location Tab – This tab is used to enter information about the violation location.
F. Violator Tab – This tab is used to enter information about the violator.
G. License Tab – This tab is used to enter operator license information.

H. Vehicle Tab – This tab is used to enter vehicle information.
I. Violation Tab – This tab is used to enter violation and charge information.
J. Notes Tab – Used if additional facts are available to support the charge. This information
will be visible on the violator’s copy.
K. Officer Tab – This tab is used to confirm or modify officer data.
L. Court Tab – This tab is used to confirm court data.
M. Remarks Tab – This tab is used to enter officer remarks which will not appear on the
violator’s printed ticket.
N. Witness Tab – This tab is used to enter witness data, if needed.
O. Employment Tab – This tab is used to enter violator’s employment data, if needed
P. Photos Tab – This tab is not used by this department.
Q. Select “Save and Print” or “Print and Copy” if multiple tickets are to be issued.
R. Statistical traffic analysis information will be electronically transmitted to REJIS once
manually entered by the issuing officer.
IV. MOBILE TICKETING - WRITTEN WARNINGS
If the officer determines that a citation is not an appropriate action for a traffic offense, he/she
may instead issue the violator a written warning through the Mobile Ticketing system. The
action is like issuing a citation:
A. Log-in to the system utilizing the officer’s REJIS identification number and password.
B. Verify that the shift settings are current.
C. When entering a new ticket, choose the violation type (Local Ordinance) and then
appropriate Ticket Type from the drop-down menu (Citation OR Parking)
D. Start on the “Basics” tab. Required fields are labeled according to REJIS standards but
available information should be entered.
E. Location Tab – This tab is used to enter information about the violation location.
F. Violator Tab – This tab is used to enter information about the violator.

G. License Tab – This tab is used to enter operator license information.
H. Vehicle Tab – This tab is used to enter vehicle information.
I. Violation Tab – This tab is used to enter violation category. Other information related to

V. HAND-WRITTEN MISSOURI UNIFORM COMPLAINT AND SUMMONS:
A. Operate, Park, A.C.M.V, with HazMat boxes: Check “Operate” for traffic violations,
check “Park” for parking violations, check “A.C.M.V.” and/or “with HazMat” if the
vehicle is a commercial motor vehicle or has a hazardous materials placard(s).
B. Ord/RSMo boxes: The ordinance number is required unless the violator is being charged
in state court then the RSMo is required.
C. Complaint number: If the violator is physically arrested, the complaint number of the
written report that corresponds to the arrest shall be written on the summons.
D. Court Date: Specify an appearance on the court date as noted specifically by the
Municipal Court; “To Be Notified” (TBN) for State Court and St. Louis County Family
Court.
E. Court time is 6:00 PM for Municipal Court; “To Be Notified” (TBN) for State Court and
St. Louis County Family Court.
F. Crash involvement: Write the correct report number on the summons and check the "in
accident" box.
G. DWI/BAC: Check the ‘DWI/BAC’ box if the violator is charged with a DWI/BAC
related offense.
H. Officer's Signature
I. Prosecutor’s Signature: Leave blank unless ticket is for a juvenile under 15 years of age.
In that case, enter “Family Court”.
J. Notes Section of Officer’s Copy: For juveniles under 15 years of age indicate:
a. Parent(s) names;

VI. PROCEDURE
1. All tickets will be reviewed by the immediate supervisor for accuracy prior to the end of
the officer’s shift. Officer copies of Mobile Ticketing entries will be placed in the Record
Clerk’s mailbox located in the Squad Room. Manually completed summonses will be
placed in the Court lockbox.
2. In the event it becomes necessary for a summons to be entered into Mobile Ticketing (if
hand-written ticket is created), amended, voided, or other action to be performed on the
ticket, the officer who issued the summons will prepare a Summons Action Request
(OPD - F1103) detailing the reasons for the action. The form will be forwarded through
the chain of command to the Deputy Chief who will forward it to Court, if needed.
3. Watch commanders, along with Court personnel, will ensure that an adequate supply of
handwritten tickets is secured in the Roll Call Room. A supervisor will issue tickets to an
officer as needed. The officer receiving the tickets will initial the logbook taking
responsibility for the tickets assigned to him/her.
4. Damaged, lost, or stolen summonses will be reported in writing immediately to the
appropriate supervisor, who will in turn investigate the matter and report their findings to
the Deputy Chief.
5. Summons charge disposition, including amendments and dismissals, is the responsibility
of the Courts Division.
6. Upon employment with this Department, officers will be issued a ticket book to be used
when Mobile Ticketing is not available. During the annual formal line inspection, ticket
books will be reviewed with Court records to ensure that no tickets are damaged, lost or
stolen.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
