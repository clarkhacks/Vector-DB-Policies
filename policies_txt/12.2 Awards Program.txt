AWARDS PROGRAMS
I. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this General Order is to establish an awards program to recognize
Department employees, other law enforcement agencies, law enforcement officials from
an outside agency and citizens who have distinguished themselves by performing some
notable act or service in the furtherance of law enforcement and community betterment.
II. DEPARTMENT EMPLOYEE AWARDS PROGRAM:
A. Medal of Honor (awarded posthumously)
Awarded by the Chief of Police to a Department employee for a conspicuous act of valor
who, while performing a police-related act on or off duty, has given his/her life. The
award shall be given to the employee's family member.
B. Distinguished Service Citation for Valor
Awarded by the Chief of Police to a Department employee who performs an act of
heroism in the face of personal injury or risk of life. This award will be evidenced by:
1. Blue Breast Bar/ Blue Lapel Pin
2. Certificate
C. Meritorious Service Citation
Awarded by the Chief of Police to a Department employee who performs an extremely
laudable or extraordinary act and shall be evidenced by the following:
1. Red Breast Bar/ Red Lapel Pin

2. Certificate
D. Chief's Commendation
Awarded by the Chief of Police to a Department employee or work element that performs
a particularly note worthy act or service based on determined and intelligent
performance. This award shall be evidenced by:
1. Green Breast Bar/ Green Lapel Pin
2. Certificate
E. Award of Excellence
Awarded by the Chief of Police to a Department employee whose actions deserve
recognition but may not warrant one of the above awards.
III. PROCEDURE FOR RECOMMENDATION FOR DEPARTMENT EMPLOYEE
AWARDS:
A. Recommendation for one of the five awards which Department employees are eligible
shall be initiated by an employee's supervisor.
B. Recommendation shall be made in memorandum form summarizing the incident for
which the employee is being recommended for recognition, as well as any police reports
or other pertinent information.
C. It shall be the responsibility of the employee's supervisor to verify the validity of the
recommendation as much as possible.
D. The recommendation shall be forwarded through the chain of command to the Chief
of Police for review and approval.
IV. CITIZEN SERVICE CITATION:
A. The Citizen Service Citation may be awarded to a citizen whose assistance to the
Police Department was given without regard to personal welfare and had positive bearing
on the outcome of an incident and contributed to the furtherance of law enforcement in
the community.
B. The Citizen Service Citation may be recommended for presentation to a citizen by an
employee of this Department. The procedures for recommendation for this award shall
conform to those stated in Section III of this Order.
C. The citizen shall receive a certificate recognizing his/her action and a blue lapel pin.

V. CERTIFICATE OF APPRECIATION:
The Certificate of Appreciation may be awarded to another law enforcement agency or to
a member of another law enforcement agency that has performed an outstanding act of
service for the Olivette Police Department. The act may be singular or involve
consistently superior achievements to the community and the criminal justice system. The
award shall be recognized by a certificate.
VI. CERTIFICATE OF RECOGNITION:
The Certificate of Recognition may be awarded to a citizen or organization who has
demonstrated dedication to the community by a singular act or a series of acts that are
significant note to the betterment of the community and the Olivette Police Department.
VII. PRESENTATION OF AWARDS:
A. All awards, except the Award of Excellence, shall be presented by the Chief of Police.
B. The Award of Excellence shall be made by the employee's commanding officer.
C. A copy of the award shall be placed in the employee's personnel file.
VIII. WEARING OF AWARDS
A. Breast bars awarded to Department employees, when worn, shall be worn by uniform
personnel on the top seam of the left breast pocket and centered on the outermost garment
of the field uniform.
B. Lapel pins awarded to Department employees, when worn, shall be worn on the left
lapel of the sport or suit coat.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
