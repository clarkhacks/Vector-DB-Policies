POLICE ESCORTS
I. PURPOSE:
It is the purpose of this General Order to establish procedures for performing the different
types of police escorts.
II. EMERGENCY ESCORTS:
Officers traveling in proximity to other emergency vehicles, such as fire trucks and
ambulances, responding to the same scene, should keep a safe distance from other
emergency vehicles so to allow civilian motorists to be able to discern between the
emergency vehicles and yield accordingly.
Escorting privately owned vehicles under emergency circumstances should be avoided
unless authorized by the Watch Commander.
In medical emergencies, the patient should be transported by ambulance when possible.
Emergency lights and siren will be used at all times when an emergency escort is
authorized by the Watch Commander.
III. NON-EMERGENCY ESCORTS:
A. Non-emergency escorts may warrant the use of emergency lights and siren to escort
vehicles through intersections, traffic, or crowds.
B. Non-emergency escorts will be made at or below the posted speed limits, and in a safe
and prudent manner.
C. Officers may provide “non-emergency” escorts for the following:
1. Public officials,
