COMMAND PROTOCOL
I. POLICY
It is the policy of the Olivette Police Department to provide uniform direction to all
Police Department employees through an established Chain of Command in order to
promote consistency, reduce confusion and enhance public safety.
II. PROCEDURE
A. In normal day-to-day operations, the following command structure is in place.
1. The Chief of Police represents the highest-ranking authority within the Police
Department.
2. The Deputy Chief represents the second highest ranking authority within the
Police Department.
3. The Lieutenants are the highest-ranking officers within the different platoons.
4. Supervisors (Sergeants or Corporals) are the highest-ranking officers within a
platoon when a Lieutenant is not on duty. Shift commanders and supervisors
serve as the “Chief’s Representative” and have overall command authority of the
Police Department operations both at headquarters and in the field after hours
(1700 to 0800) and on weekends (Saturday and Sunday).
B. The protocol for command succession will be as follows:
1. From Chief of Police to the designated Acting Chief of Police, the Deputy
Chief

3. From Supervisor (Sergeant or Corporal) to the designated Acting Supervisor
C. All personnel will immediately comply with the lawful direction provided by an
officer or employee of the supervisory rank. Failure to do so may be considered
insubordination.
D. All Supervisors and Commanders retain full authority and responsibility for their
positions until relieved by an authorized person.
E. Supervisors and Commanders will name a subordinate to acting authority whenever
the Supervisor or Commander cannot be physically present and able to assume command
functions.
G. Acting Sergeants and Commanders have full authority and responsibility of the
position they assume until relieved by their Supervisor or another superior officer.
H. Incident Command Protocol/Exceptional Situations
1. In an exceptional situation, catastrophic event, major crime, or other large-scale
event, an officer representing police command rank (at least the rank of Sergeant)
will assume command of the police operations as Incident Commander.
a. The on-duty Supervisor will assume initial responsibility and will
have overall command authority over the operation and management of
the incident until formally relieved by higher authority.
b. Transition of command over an incident from one person to another will
be formally declared to all personnel involved in the operation.
c. In situations involving personnel of different functions involved in a
single operation, the Chief of Police will designate the officer in charge. In
the absence of the Chief of Police, the highest-ranking officer on the scene
will assume the role of scene commander, until relieved by the Chief of
Police or a higher-ranking officer.
1) Exception: Incidents of officers involved in deadly force or
serious physical injury will be investigated by the Bureau of
Investigations. The Bureau of Investigations Supervisor will be
Incident Commander in these situations unless otherwise specified
by the Deputy Chief or Chief of Police.
I. Any written directive of the Olivette Police Department that assigns authority to a
specific position (i.e. Chief of Police, Deputy Chief, Commander of the Bureau of
Investigation, etc.) shall mean that position or his/her designee.
