DRUG TAKE BACK PROGRAM
I. PURPOSE
The goal of this program is twofold. The prime reason is to keep unused or expired
medications out of the hands of people who may abuse them. The second reason for the
program is to provide a safe, environmentally friendly way to dispose of unused or
expired prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) drugs. The preferred method of
incineration keeps the drugs out of landfills and groundwater. This program will
supplement the Olivette Police Department’s participation in the United States Drug
Enforcement Agency’s (DEA) bi-annual Regional Prescription Drug Take Back Program.
This program is only for unused or expired prescription and OTC drugs, not illegal drugs.
The collection and disposal of unused or expired prescription and OTC drugs must be
conducted under specific guidelines set forth by the DEA. The guidelines require that
law enforcement agency personnel are the primary component of any collection effort.
The Department has participated in several DEA sponsored “Drug Take Back” collection
days. However, the purpose of installing a drop box at this Command is to provide a
continuous and convenient option for the safe disposal of unused or expired prescription
and OTC drugs all year round.
II. DEFINITIONS
A. DROP BOX – a through-wall metal style door located in the main hallway of the
Olivette City Center near the Police Department Records window. The drop box door
allows items to be dropped in, but not retrieved through the same opening. For retrieval
of items, the rear of the drop box is accessible from a secure room within the Police
Department. The key to the secure room will be maintained by the Deputy Chief and the
Evidence Custodian.
B. PRESCRIPTION DRUGS – those medications prescribed by a physician to an
individual. They may include pills, tablets, liquids, or other related medications.

C. OVER-THE-COUNTER (OTC) DRUGS – those medications available to individuals
that may be purchased from a retail location. They may include pills, tablets, liquids, or
other related medications.
D. ILLEGAL DRUGS – those drugs not prescribed by a physician or available OTC,
commonly known as street drugs (i.e. Marijuana, Cocaine, Methamphetamine, Heroine,
etc.)
E. PROHIBITED ITEMS – illegal drugs, bio-hazardous materials, radioactive material,
sharps (i.e. needles/syringes), inhalers.
F. PRIVATE INDIVIDUALS – individuals at least 18 years of age. This does not
include pharmacies, pharmaceutical sales representatives, physician offices, hospitals or
any other commercial entity. Commercial businesses are specifically prohibited from
using the program to dispose of their unused or expired drugs.
III. PROCEDURE
A. Program Operation
1. The drop box will be properly marked as Drug Take Back Drop Box. The drop
box will be continuously monitored by video surveillance.
2. The drop box is available to private individuals during normal business hours
of the City Center.
3. When a private individual brings unused or expired drugs to this Command for
disposal, he/she shall put the drugs into the drop box.
4. On the first available working day of each week, the Evidence Custodian will
check the drop box to see if it needs to be emptied.
5. If the drop box needs to be emptied, the Evidence Custodian will enter the
secure room, remove the items from the rear of the drop box and package the
items in boxes. The filled boxes will remain in the secure room until transferred to
the DEA for incineration.
6. When needed, the Evidence Custodian will arrange for the drugs to be
transferred to the DEA for incineration.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
