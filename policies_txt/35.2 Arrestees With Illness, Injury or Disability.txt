ARRESTEES WITH ILLNESS, INJURY OR DISABILITY
I. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this General Order is to establish guidelines for handling arrestees with
illnesses, injuries or disabilities.
The temporary holding cells are not intended for or equipped to handle arrestees who
require immediate or sustained medical attention. The Olivette Police Department relies
upon the Olivette Fire Department to provide emergency medical services and
transportation of such cases.
II. NOTIFICATION:
The shift commander shall be notified of all injured prisoners, or prisoners claiming
illness or injury.
III. RESISTING ARREST:
All persons that resist arrest will be examined by medical personnel, if:
1. The arrestee complains of injury or,
2. In the arresting officer’s opinion, the arrestee needs medical attention.
IV. PROCEDURES:
A. All visible pre-existing injuries, bruises or other notable signs of incapacitation will be
noted on the booking sheet.

B. Arrestees shall not be held for interrogation or other purposes if they have injuries or
illnesses that require hospitalization or immediate attention of a health care professional.
C. If a prisoner exhibits any of the following, special attention must be given while that
individual is in a holding cell:
1. Suffer from extreme alcohol intoxication or possible drug overdose, or
2. Exhibit symptoms of severe mental disorder, or
3. Have talked about committing suicide or shown signs of being a suicide risk
D. If the severity of medical conditions is unclear or if a prisoner requests medical
attention, the Olivette Fire Department shall be summoned immediately by ECDC to
evaluate the prisoner and provide the appropriate emergency treatment. If necessary, the
person shall be transported as soon as possible to a designated medical care provider.
E. A police officer will convey the prisoner, if the illness or injuries are not serious/life
threatening to the appropriate healthcare facility and will be responsible for the security
of the prisoner. If the illness or injury appears to be, or is determined by the Olivette Fire
Department to be serious/life threatening, the Olivette Fire Department will provide
transportation to the appropriate medical facility. An officer will ride in the ambulance.
1. Injuries or illness not requiring immediate medical attention (as determined by
hospital staff) shall be documented by attaching the Fit for Confinement to the
arrestee’s booking sheet. The Fit for Confinement should not be scanned into
CARE Scanned Documents or attached to the police report. Additional hospital
paperwork should be placed with the prisoner’s property. The Fit for
Confinement may be copied, faxed or otherwise provided to an agency who is
assuming custody of the prisoner from this Department.
2. The original or supplemental police report shall be written concerning the
hospital transport, illness, or injury.
a. For minor charges, if the prisoner is not fit for confinement, he/she may
be released on a summons, pending warrant application, or
administratively. The Chief of Police will determine the disposition of
prisoners on more serious charges.
b. Any prisoner under the influence of alcohol or drugs, or who exhibits
violent or self-destructive behavior not requiring medical attention, will be
placed in a cell by him/herself and monitored by commissioned personnel
until transported or released.

V. PRISONER MEDICATION:
A. Prisoners who request over-the-counter (OTC) medications may be given same at the
discretion of the Watch Commander.
1. The only OTC medications that may be given to a prisoner are antacid, aspirin,
or ibuprofen.
2. The Department will keep a supply of the above mentioned OTC medications
in a secure cabinet in the juvenile holding area. Officers will not dispense their
own OTC medications to prisoners.
3. The Watch Commander will give an officer the appropriate dosage of OTC
medications as directed by the package to dispense to the prisoner. The officer
will note on the booking sheet what OTC medication was given, what time it was
given, and what dose was given.
4. The Watch Commander will complete Olivette Police Department Form #OPD
- F1166, “Prisoner OTC Medications“, to indicate what was dispensed.
B. If a prisoner is taking prescription medication, this will be noted on the booking sheet.
The booking officer will advise the Watch Commander, who will ensure that any
medication is properly administered.
C. The booking officer should determine that any medication that is with the person when
arrested, or medication later brought in by relatives, is actually what is described on the
medication container. Any discrepancies should be verified through the Olivette Fire
Department.
D. When given a dose of medication, the prisoner should be visually observed taking the
medication and this event should be documented on the Booking Sheet Prisoner Log.
E. If a prisoner returns to the holding facility after being seen at a medical facility for a
Fit for Confinement, and he/she has received a prescription that needs to be filled, the
following procedure will apply:
1. If the prisoner has valid health insurance, his/her health insurance information
should be obtained and used to process the prescription. Any remaining amount
(i.e. copays) should be processed using the pharmacy voucher.
2. The watch commander will ensure the voucher for a Department-approved
pharmacy is accurately completed.
3. An officer will respond to a Department-approved pharmacy with the voucher
and the prisoner’s prescription, which the pharmacy will fill.

4. The officer filling the prescription will obtain a receipt for the medication(s)
obtained at the pharmacy.
5. Even if the prescription is for more than 2 days’ worth of medicine, the officer
should advise the pharmacy to only fill the prescription for 2 days. If there are
additional days remaining on the prescription, the officer will advise the prisoner
he/she will need to obtain a new prescription for those days. Some medications
may not be able to be divided into 2 day doses (i.e. small bottle of laxative,
inhaler, etc.) In those instances, obtain the smallest dose possible (at least 2
days).
6. The watch commander will ensure that Form #OPD - 1157, Prisoner
Medication, is accurately completed.
7. Form #OPD - 1157 and the pharmacy receipt will be forwarded to the Bureau
of Support Services Commander. A copy of the pharmacy receipt should be
attached to the prisoner’s booking sheet.
8. When administering medication to a prisoner, follow steps A, B, and D above.
9. If the amount of the medication(s) is over $50.00 total, prior authorization is
required from the Chief of Police.
VI. REPORTS:
All injuries, or claimed injuries, which occurred to arrestees while in the custody of this
Department, must be reported in detail in an Olivette CARE report. The report will
include:
A. Full pedigree of subject,
B. Extent of injury as observed by the officer,
C. Any statements made by the subject concerning the injury,
D. Location where treated,
E. Doctor’s diagnosis of the injury; be specific, “treated and released” is not sufficient,
F. The signed Fit for Confinement form attached to the booking sheet (if applicable).
G. The Shift Supervisor will ensure that a copy of the report is forwarded to the Chief of
Police.
H. In those cases where an arrestee is injured or complains of injury, the report will
include photographs of the prisoner showing all injuries or lack of injuries.

VII. DEATHS, SUICIDES AND ATTEMPTED SUICIDES:
A. All cases of prisoner death, suicide or attempted suicide will be immediately reported
to the shift commander/supervisor.
B. Emergency medical personnel will be summoned.
C. The Medical Examiner will be notified as soon as possible as necessary.
D. All in-custody deaths or serious injuries will be investigated by an outside agency.
VII. TRANSPORTING PRISONERS FROM OTHER AGENCIES & FACILITIES:
When accepting prisoners from another agency, the watch commander may use discretion
if:
A. The prisoner has an injury or illness (even if a Fit for Confinement has been obtained
by the originating agency);
B. The prisoner is currently taking prescription medication but has no way of obtaining
the required medication.
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
