SELECTION PROCESS
I. PURPOSE
The purpose of this General Order is to establish a selection process for the hiring of
cadets, commissioned, and non-commissioned employees to the Olivette Police
Department.
II. SELECTION PROCESS FOR COMMISSIONED EMPLOYEES
A. Step 1 – Resume Submission
Candidates for the position of Police Officer will submit a resume to the Human
Resources Administrator. Resumes will be reviewed by the Human Resources
Administrator and the Chief of Police to determine if the necessary qualifications are met
as outlined in the job announcement for the candidate to move forward in the hiring
process.
B. Step 2 – City of Olivette Application
Candidates for the position of Police Officer chosen to move forward in the process will
be required to submit the City of Olivette employment application. The application will
be completed via online or paper version prior to completion of the written examination.
C. Step 3 – Written Examination
1. Candidates will be scheduled to take a written examination designed to
measure the candidates’ aptitude and suitability for a career in law enforcement.
2. Candidates must score a minimum of 70% or above to continue in the selection
process.

D. Step 4 – City of Olivette Police Department Application
All candidates moving forward in the process will be required to complete and return the
following documents prior to the completion of the interview panel:
1. City of Olivette Police Department Application
2. Certificate of Applicant and Authorization for Release of Information
The City of Olivette Police Department Application will be reviewed to determine if the
candidate meets the Department qualifications for the position of Police Officer. If it is
determined that an individual does not meet the qualifications, the candidate will be
informed in writing by the Human Resources Administrator that they will no longer
move forward in the hiring process.
E. Step 5 – Interview Panel
1. Candidates who have achieved the minimum score or above on the written test
will be interviewed by a panel selected by the Chief of Police. The panel will
consist of a minimum of three (3) members, with a minimum of one (1) of whom
is from outside the Department.
2. Candidates interviewed must achieve a minimum score of 70% and a
recommendation from the Interview Panel to continue the hiring process.
3. Recommended candidates will be forwarded to the Chief of Police for
consideration of an interview. If applicable, only the top three (3) candidates will
be forwarded to the Chief of Police for consideration of an interview.
F. Step 6 – Police Chief Interview
1. The Chief of Police and Deputy Chief will interview the candidates.
2. The Chief of Police may make a recommendation to the City Manager
regarding a conditional offer of employment.
3. Upon City Manager approval, a conditional offer of employment will be
extended to the candidate. The offer is conditional upon the results of a
background investigation, CVSA, physical examination/drug screen, physical
abilities assessment, and a psychological evaluation.
G. Step 7 – Background Investigation
1. The Bureau of Investigations will complete the background investigation,
checking in detail all information contained in the candidate’s City of Olivette
Police Department Application, and any information uncovered during previous
interviews. The background investigation will consist of a criminal history check,

and a verification of the following including, but not limited to qualifying
credentials, educational achievements, employment history, personal/professional
references, and a review of relevant national or state decertification resources, if
available.
2. If it is determined that the candidate does not meet the Department
qualifications as outlined in this policy or any falsifications, omissions and/or
misrepresentations are uncovered at this point, the candidate will be eliminated
from the selection process and will be notified in writing by the Human Resources
Administrator that the conditional offer of employment is rescinded.
H. Step 8 – Computer Voice Stress Analyzer (CVSA)
1. The candidate will be scheduled for a Computer Voice Stress Analyzer
(CVSA) examination and will be given a Pre-Employment CVSA Booklet to
complete.
a. This examination will be conducted by a trained and certified
Department CVSA examiner.
b. The CVSA examiner will be provided with all information compiled
through the background investigation.
c. The results of the CVSA examination will be considered confidential.
The CVSA examiner’s report will be considered by the Chief of Police
and the City Manager.
I. Step 9 – Medical Examination/Drug Screen and Physical Abilities Assessment
1. The candidate will be scheduled for both a medical examination/drug screen
and a physical abilities assessment. The City of Olivette will assume financial
responsibility for the exams. The physical abilities assessment may be waived for
any candidate who can demonstrate successful completion of a physical abilities
assessment within the last year by a Missouri P.O.S.T. approved training provider.
2. If the candidate is unable to pass the medical examination/drug screen or the
physical abilities assessment, the conditional offer of employment will be
rescinded.
J. Step 10 – Psychological Examination
1. The candidate will be scheduled for a psychological examination. The City of
Olivette will assume financial responsibility for the exam.
2. If the psychiatrist does not recommend the candidate for the position of Police
Officer, the conditional offer of employment will be rescinded.

K. Step 11 – Confirmation of Job Offer
Upon successful completion of all the above steps in the hiring process, a tentative
employment start date will be determined. All candidates for commissioned employee
positions must be certified and licensed by Missouri P.O.S.T. prior to appointment as
Police Officer.
III. SELECTION PROCESS FOR POLICE CADET
A. Step 1 – Resume Submission
Candidates for the position of Police Cadet will submit a resume to the Human Resources
Administrator. Resumes will be reviewed by the Human Resources Administrator and the
Chief of Police to determine if the necessary qualifications are met as outlined in the job
announcement for the candidate to move forward in the hiring process.
B. Step 2 – City of Olivette Application
Candidates for the position of Police Cadet chosen to move forward in the process will be
required to submit the City of Olivette employment application. The application will be
completed via online or paper version prior to completion of physical fitness test.
C. Step 3 - Physical Fitness Test
1. Candidates will be required to pass a physical fitness test to ensure they are
able to complete the physical training requirements of the St. Louis County and
Municipal Police Academy or other chosen approved training provider.
2. Candidates must pass all sections of the test according to the standards of the
St. Louis County and Municipal Police Academy or other chosen approved
training provider.
3. If the candidate fails to successfully complete any section, he/she will be
informed of their dismissal from the selection process and will be required to
leave the testing area.
D. Step 4 – Written Examination
1. Candidates will be scheduled to take a written examination designed to
measure the candidates’ aptitude and suitability for a career in law enforcement.
2. Candidates must score 70% or above in each section to continue in the
selection process.
E. Step 5 – City of Olivette Police Department Application
All candidates moving forward in the process will be required to complete and return the

L. Step 12 - Successful completion of Police Academy
The cadet must successfully complete all requirements for graduation from the St. Louis
County and Municipal Police Academy, or other approved training provider, including
documentation of successful completion of a physical abilities assessment.
Documentation of completion must be provided to the city prior to receiving his/her
commission as an Olivette Police Officer. Failure to complete any requirement for
graduation from the St. Louis County and Municipal Police Academy, or other approved
training provider, will result in termination of employment from the City of Olivette.
M. Step 13 - Successful completion of Missouri P.O.S.T. Examination
The cadet must successfully complete the Missouri P.O.S.T. Examination prior to
receiving his/her commission as an Olivette Police Officer.
N. Step 14 – Confirmation of Job Offer
Upon successful completion of all the above steps in the hiring process, a start date will
be determined for the cadet’s appointment as a commissioned Police Officer with the
City of Olivette.
IV. ELIGIBILITY LIST
An eligibility list from a selection process for both commissioned officers and cadets will
be maintained by the Human Resources Department for one (1) year from the date of
application.
V. RE-APPLICATION AND TESTING
Candidates that are eliminated from a selection process are eligible to re-apply one (1)
year from the date of their application.
