COMMUNITY POLICING AND PROBLEM SOLVING
I. PURPOSE
The Community Policing concept is to provide a means of coordinating and improving
the effectiveness and efficiency of police services in an established area. Effective
communication and cooperation between the patrol officers, the Community Engagement
Officer and the Bureau of Investigations will greatly enhance the success of this team
approach to solving community problems.
Simply stated, Community Policing is a philosophy that says interacting with, and
working as full partners with the community to reduce crime and improve the quality of
life makes better sense than ignoring people’s needs, interests, and input.
Positive community relations are the daily responsibility of all members of the
Department, commissioned and non-commissioned alike. All employees assigned to the
Olivette Police Department will be involved in the Community Policing Philosophy. The
Department is charged with constantly improving our practices as they relate to
community involvement.
II. PATROL OFFICERS’ RESPONSIBILITIES
A. Seek to improve professional policing by using a proactive approach which involves
an analytic effort. Problem oriented policing assumes that many crimes are fostered by
persistent problems in a community. All officers are expected to address both criminal
and non-criminal matters affecting the citizens of Olivette.
B. Patrol officers, when not on radio assignments, shall create an effective working
partnership between the community and the police.

C. Where feasible, foot or bicycle patrols are established to increase interaction between
the police and neighborhood residents and to enhance the citizen’s sense of access to the
Department.
D. Attend and help organize Neighborhood Watch meetings and other neighborhood
meetings that have been set by subdivision trustees or residents.
E. Work directly in the community as outreach specialists and problem-solvers by taking
an innovative, self-disciplined, and self-motivated approach.
F. Report crime patterns that can be addressed using additional Department resources or
special enforcement activities to the Watch Commander.
G. Identify traffic enforcement sites from citizens’ complaints, high accident locations,
and hazardous driving locations.
H. Ensure homes are checked when listed on the “Vacation Watch” list.
I. Identify and notify owners of open garage doors and other security concerns discovered
during the midnight shift.
J. Distribute crime prevention literature to crime victims (i.e., Home Safety, Domestic
Violence, Neighborhood Watch, Identity Theft, etc.)
III. COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT OFFICER
While crime prevention and community involvement are the responsibility of all
members of the Department in order to maintain a pro-active approach to the prevention
and suppression of crime, the Community Engagement Officer will have a primary
responsibility of implementing and managing crime prevention and community
involvement programs. The Community Engagement Officer is assigned to the Bureau
of Support Services.
Responsibilities may include, but are not limited to:
A. Identify and maintain a list of Neighborhood Subdivision Trustees.
B. Discuss with Watch Commanders and Patrol Officers issues and concerns of various
neighborhood groups.
C. Coordinate and give community education presentations on a variety of topics which
may include but is not limited to:
1. Personal Safety
2. Elder Adult Safety

4. Child Safety

D. Create social media content and announcements to publicize the objectives, problems,
and successes of the Department’s community involvement efforts.
E. Report to the command staff for action on all feedback, comments, suggestions, or

F. Liaison with the City’s Communications Manager.

J. Organize, attend, and/or present at community engagement initiatives including, but
not limited to, Coffee with a Cop and the Citizen’s Police Academy.
