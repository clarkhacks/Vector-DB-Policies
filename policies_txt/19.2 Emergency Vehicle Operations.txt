EMERGENCY VEHICLE OPERATIONS
I. PURPOSE:
The purpose of this General Order is to establish policy and procedure for emergency
vehicle operation by the Olivette Police Department in other than “hot pursuit” situations
and to guide officers in utilizing Department vehicles in a safe and efficient manner. This
directive shall govern an officer’s conduct in the evaluation of situations which may
require the extraordinary operation of a Department vehicle. Officers are, at all times,
subject to the provision of this General Order.
II. DEFINITIONS:
A. Emergency Driving: Driving pursuant to police calls such as “Officer in Need of
Aid”, “Crime in progress”, “Accidents with Injury”, etc. During emergency driving, the
officer is not pursuing another vehicle and thus has greater control over the situation.
III. POLICY:
Public safety and protection of human life are our paramount concerns. A need to
apprehend a law violator or to respond to a location or situation does not normally justify
creation of new or additional risks of injury or death to police officers or others.
Occasionally, the need to apprehend the serious criminal or to provide emergency
services may justify driving outside normally applicable law and rules of the road.
However, State law creates only limited exceptions for emergency vehicle operation. It
is the policy of this Department that police vehicle operation shall be within the law and
vehicles shall be operated in a reasonable manner and with due regard for the rights and
safety of others. Irresponsible, careless and reckless driving is prohibited and will not be

tolerated. Police vehicle operation shall be at all times consistent with the concept of
“reasonable safety” and with other requirements of this order.
Police vehicles are considered emergency vehicles only when being operated in
accordance with RSMo 304.022 by displaying both flashing red lights, and audible siren.
Officers operating emergency vehicles are required to exercise reasonable care under the
circumstances for the emergency, to prevent injury to himself/herself or others.
IV. CODE 3
A. Any commissioned officer is authorized to activate lights and siren to respond only
when it is reasonably necessary to accomplish one of the following:
1. To prevent the commission of a crime dangerous to life (i.e., robbery in
progress, shooting, flourishing a gun). Code 3 operation is not authorized for
responding to peace disturbance calls unless there is the element of serious peril
to human life;
2. To provide emergency assistance to persons exposed to imminent threat of
death or serious physical injury (i.e., accident with injuries, haz-mat spill);
3. To apprehend a felon in the commission of a crime;
4. To avert or minimize the effect of a public catastrophe or emergency involving
the threat of major property damage (i.e., fire or explosion);
5. Medical calls for service;
6. To stop a traffic violator to take enforcement action;
7. To stop a vehicle, based on reasonable suspicion, to conduct further
investigation;
8. To initiate pursuit that meets the criteria outlined in General Order 19.5.
V. CODE 1
Calls for service not listed as Code 3 are Code 1 calls. Field units will respond without
the use of emergency lights and siren and in compliance with all traffic regulations.
VI. SUPERVISOR’S RESPONSIBILITY
It is the responsibility of the supervisor to be aware of conditions or situations that would
dictate a lesser response and direct officers accordingly.
VII. EMERGENCY STOPPING OR PARKING

In accordance with RSMo 304.022 an authorized police vehicle may stop or park
contrary to posted parking regulations in bona fide emergency situations only. Under
emergency situations the police vehicle must display a flashing red light as a warning to
approaching motorists. If in the discretion of the officer, based upon the situation, a
vehicle must be parked contrary to posted parking regulations, the officer will take into
consideration existing traffic conditions and park in as safe a manner as possible.
VIII. RESTRICTIONS ON U-TURNS
The making of a U-turn is recognized as a maneuver that is considered more dangerous
than a normal left-hand turn and is not the preferred method of changing direction. While
some situations necessitate the need for a U-turn, a greater degree of caution shall be
used. A U-turn shall not be undertaken unless the movement can be made in safety
and without interfering with other traffic (RSMo 304.341). The driver shall be alert
to other traffic, both approaching from the front and from the rear. The driver shall signal
a left turn prior to making the U-turn to alert any approaching vehicles of the impending
movement. The safety of all traffic lies solely in the hands of the person making the U-
turn. This applies to emergency and non-emergency situations.
Even when operating in an emergency capacity, no statute will relieve the driver of an
authorized emergency vehicle from the duty to drive with due regard for the safety
of all persons, nor shall such provisions protect the driver from the consequences of
his reckless disregard for the safety of others (RSMo 300.100).
This General Order supersedes all prior directives and orders inconsistent therewith.
